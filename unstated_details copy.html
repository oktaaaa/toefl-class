"Unstated Detail" questions are like a reverse detail question. Instead of finding what *is* in the passage, your job is to find what is not. These questions are easy to spot because they always contain the word NOT or EXCEPT.

Your Mission: The passage is the crime scene. The four answer choices are suspects. Three are telling the truth (their statements are in the passage). One is lying (their statement is not mentioned or is contradicted). Your job is to find the liar!

A 4-Step Strategy

  1. Identify the Question: Spot the keyword **NOT** or **EXCEPT**. This tells you to look for the one answer that isn't supported by the text.
  2. Read the Answer Choices: Quickly read the four options first. This gives you specific details to look for in the passage.
  3. Scan and Eliminate: Take one answer choice at a time and scan the passage to find it. If you find evidence for it, it's a *true* statement, so you can eliminate it.
  4. Isolate the Answer: The one choice you cannot find evidence for is the correct answer. It's the "unstated detail."

Tips and Common Traps

  • Watch for Paraphrasing: The answer choice might use synonyms or rephrased ideas from the passage (e.g., "biggest planet" vs. "largest planet").
  • Use ONLY the Passage: An answer might be a true fact in the real world, but if it's not in the passage, it's an "unstated detail." Don't use outside knowledge!
  • Process of Elimination is Key: If you're stuck, focus on finding the three *true* statements. The one left over must be the answer.
