Understanding Negative Statements

In the TOEFL Listening section, a common pattern is for a speaker to make a negative statement. The correct answer choice is often a positive statement that expresses the same idea. The key is to listen for the negative and find the answer that uses a positive or opposite word to restate the idea.

1. Negatives with Opposite Meanings

The speaker says something is not one way, which means it is the opposite.

Example (What you hear) Correct Answer (The meaning) Negative Expression
Tom is not sad about the results. Tom is happy about the results. not sad = happy
The class isn't difficult. The class is easy. not difficult = easy

2. Negatives with Logical Equivalents

Sometimes, a negative verb phrase has a single positive verb that means the same thing.

Example (What you hear) Correct Answer (The meaning) Negative Expression
Steve did not pass the class. Steve failed the class. did not pass = failed
She does not remember the date. She has forgotten the date. does not remember = forgotten
